SAMPLE WORKOUT PLAN

Here's a sample 4-week Pre-Phase workout plan designed for building overall strength and muscle

PRE-PHASE OVERVIEW (WEEKS 1-4)

OBJECTIVE

Build overall strength and muscle, accumulate enough muscle mass to aid in weight loss, and prepare for Phase I.

WHAT TO EXPECT

Better coordination, increased strength, correction of muscle imbalances and improvement in exercise form.

LENGTH OF PHASE

4 weeks

HOW THE PROGRAM IS STRUCTURED

Weekly Schedule

You'll train 3x per week, alternating between:

  • DAY A: Upper Body Strength + Core
  • DAY B: Lower Body Strength + Glutes
  • DAY C: Mobility & Recovery Focus

Each day has a clear structure: Warm-Up → Main Workout → Core Finisher

Weekly Progression

Each week, we progress the training slightly to keep your body adapting:

  • 1
    Week 1

    Build foundation, learn form, lighter loads

  • 2
    Week 2

    Improve endurance, slightly increase volume

  • 3
    Week 3

    Peak volume, more reps and challenge

  • 4
    Week 4

    Deload in reps, but increase resistance

SAMPLE WORKOUT DAYS

DAY A

Upper Body Strength + Core

Warm-Up:
  • Treadmill Walk - 5 min
  • Arm Circles - 10 reps each direction
  • Wall Angels - 10 reps
Main Workout:
  • Elevated Push-Ups - 2 sets x 3-8 reps
  • Machine Incline Press - 3 sets x 10-12 reps
  • Dumbbell Bent Over Row - 3 sets x 10-12 reps
  • Lat Pulldown - 3 sets x 12-15 reps
Core Finisher:
  • Bird Dog - 3 sets x 12 reps
  • Isometric Planks - 3 sets x 30 seconds
DAY B

Lower Body Strength + Glutes

Warm-Up:
  • Treadmill Walk - 5 min
  • Hip Circles - 10 reps each direction
  • Bodyweight Squats - 10 reps
Main Workout:
  • Goblet Squats - 3 sets x 10-12 reps
  • Dumbbell Romanian Deadlift - 3 sets x 10-12 reps
  • Leg Press Machine - 3 sets x 10-12 reps
  • Glute Bridge with Dumbbell - 3 sets x 12-15 reps
Core Finisher:
  • Dead Bug - 3 sets x 12 reps each side
  • Isometric Planks - 3 sets x 30 seconds
DAY C

Mobility & Recovery Focus

Warm-Up:
  • Treadmill Walk - 5 min
  • Arm Swings - 10 reps each direction
  • Leg Swings (Front & Side) - 10 reps each leg
Active Mobility Flow:
  • Cat-Cow Spinal Mobility - 2 sets x 10 reps
  • World's Greatest Stretch - 2 sets x 5 reps each side
  • 90/90 Hip Rotations - 2 sets x 8 reps each side
Static Stretching:
  • Shoulder Stretch - 30 seconds each side
  • Hamstring Stretch (Seated) - 30 seconds each side
  • Child's Pose - 45 seconds

KEY CONCEPTS

BMR

Basal Metabolic Rate: Number of calories your body burns at rest

TDEE

Total Daily Energy Expenditure: Includes all daily activity to show how many calories you burn in a day

WEIGHT LOSS PRINCIPLE

Consume fewer calories than your TDEE to create a calorie deficit, causing your body to burn fat for energy

ESSENTIAL WARM-UP EXERCISES

1. Hip Adductor Stretch

Targets inner thigh muscles. Hold for up to 30 seconds, repeat 3 times.

30 second hold

2. Straight Leg Raise

Builds strength in thigh muscles. Raise leg 8 inches from floor.

2 sets x 15 reps

3. Resisted Hip Flexion

Builds strength in thighs using resistance band.

2 sets x 15 reps

4. Pull-Apart

Upper back exercise using resistance band.

6-10 reps

5. Incline Push-Ups

Modified push-ups using bench or counter.

6-10 reps

Myofascial release for various muscle groups.

30-60 seconds per area

PROGRAM GOALS & FOCUS AREAS

Muscle Building & Toning

We prioritize resistance training with dumbbells, barbells, and cables, using a mix of compound movements and isolation exercises to help you:

  • Improve muscle tone and definition
  • Build lean muscle mass (which increases metabolism)
  • Improve posture, balance, and daily strength

Core Strength & Stability

Each session ends with a core finisher to build trunk stability, reduce risk of injury, and flatten your midsection:

  • Functional core moves (not just sit-ups)
  • Planks, bird dogs, hanging knee raises
  • Train your core the way it's meant to function
